## Blocking findings resolved during review

### Continuous history could be reconstructed from existence-only facts

The initial implementation could restore `mutation_history_coverage=full` after an unowned A→B→A net-zero mutation, after an episode change, or after an orphan completion interleaved with an already-open lease.

**Resolution**: full coverage is now a continuous baseline-to-current chain of exhaustive, operation-scoped leases. Artifact revision, degraded intervals, episode boundaries, orphan receipts, exact effective checkpoints, and unique lease operation identities are projection invariants. Join/resume append an explicit event-sourced boundary interval.

### Invalid coverage events could be appended before reducer rejection

`commitTaskCommand` previously appended an authority record before evolving the projection. A strict Pre after an episode boundary could therefore persist an event that replay would reject and wedge the repository.

**Resolution**: every decision is reducer-preflighted; production repeats `evolveAll` and `assertV3TaskProjection` with exact persisted timestamps and task sequences before `commitRecord` appends any authority bytes.

### Core and derived coverage folds could disagree

A legacy/hand-built `task_joined` stream or artifact-changing `artifact_reconciled` event without a following coverage event degraded core state but left outcome and ledger telemetry at `full`.

**Resolution**: core, outcome projection, and ledger all downgrade mutation/prewrite coverage at lifecycle and artifact-delta boundaries. A valid later `coverage_changed` event in the same controlled transaction may restore the proven value.

### Completion receipts were not fully bound to authorization

The same operation could authorize `patch` and later accept a `shell` completion, or accept a completion from another host profile, while retaining full coverage.

**Resolution**: Contract 6 completion projection now retains `tool_family`; reducer and projection validation bind both `tool_family` and `host_profile` to the operation authorization and capability interval.

## Advisory findings

### A1 — installer compatibility replay is still hand-written

`install.mjs` retains a minimal lifecycle replay instead of reusing the full event authority/reducer. It is covered by compatibility and rollback tests but remains a long-term drift surface.

### A2 — coverage downgrade semantics are implemented in three folds

The authoritative engine, CLI ledger, and HOME outcome projector each express coverage downgrade rules. Cross-layer regressions now cover episode and artifact-delta boundaries, but a shared transition vocabulary would reduce future drift risk.
